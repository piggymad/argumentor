Claim Data Warrant Backing Qualifier Rebuttal

Mini-lessons by component

1. Claim

The position you are defending.

A strong claim is debatable, specific, and defensible.

  • Weak: "AI is good for students."
  • Strong: "AI writing tools improve learning only when paired with explicit teacher guidance."

2. Data / Grounds

Evidence supporting the claim.

Use a mix of statistics, study citations, examples, and authorities — not all of the same kind.

  • Cite a real, traceable source — author and year: "Lam, Hew and Chiu (2018) found that…"
  • Invent neither statistics nor studies. If you cannot cite it, do not claim it.

3. Warrant

The reasoning that links data to claim.

The bridge sentence: "This shows that…", "These findings suggest that…".

Without a warrant, evidence floats.

4. Backing

Broader support for the warrant.

Show your evidence is not isolated: "This finding has been replicated by…"

5. Qualifier

How strongly the claim is stated.

Words such as generally, in most cases, often, likely. They make a claim more defensible.

6. Rebuttal

Counter-argument and your response.

Three moves: (1) name the strongest opposing view fairly, (2) concede what is genuine, (3) show why your position still holds.

Common HK ESL pitfalls

  • Subject–verb agreement — third-person singular needs an -s/-es ending.
  • a vs. an — chosen by the following sound, not letter ("an honest mistake", "a university").
  • its vs. it’s — "it’s" only ever means "it is" or "it has".
  • Wordy phrases — "in order to" → "to"; "due to the fact that" → "because".
  • Register — "a lot of" is informal; in essays prefer "many", "several", or "a substantial number of".

Theoretical framework

  • Toulmin’s Model of Argumentation (Toulmin, 2003; applied by Turós et al., 2025) — the six components used everywhere in the platform.
  • Process Writing Theory (MacArthur, 2015) — writing is recursive; revisions are versioned, not erased.
  • Sociocultural Learning Theory (Vygotsky, via Jeong et al., 2022) — the AI tutor scaffolds without doing the cognitive work.
