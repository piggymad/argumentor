How a session works

  1. Pick a topic from the curated argumentative-essay prompts, or paste your own.
  2. Draft your essay in the workspace. As you type, the right-hand panel highlights grammar issues; the left panel ticks off Toulmin components as it detects them.
  3. Ask the AI tutor when you are stuck — it asks you back instead of writing for you.
  4. Submit the draft. Your scores are saved so you can compare later attempts on the Progress page.
  5. Your teacher reviews the draft and the AI’s automated feedback, adding a final note.

Theoretical grounding

The platform implements three frameworks from the project literature review:

  • Toulmin’s Model of Argumentation (Toulmin, 2003; applied by Turós et al., 2025) — operationalised in the live component detector.
  • Process Writing Theory (MacArthur, 2015; Hayes, 2012) — drafts are versioned so revision is tracked, not erased.
  • Sociocultural Learning Theory (Vygotsky, via Jeong et al., 2022) — the AI tutor plays the “more knowledgeable other”: it scaffolds without doing the cognitive work for the learner.
